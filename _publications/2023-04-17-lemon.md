<br>
Existing middleboxes (or network functions) are scattered under their own frameworks.
To deploy them, users have to chain them using virtualization-based solutions (**slow**),
or porting them into the same framework (**impractical**).
Our key insight is that middleboxes do not need full-fledged containers to interoperate.
This is made possible by taking over their original I/O and memory interfaces,
effectively creating an intra-process lightweight virtualization environment,
specifically for middleboxes.
